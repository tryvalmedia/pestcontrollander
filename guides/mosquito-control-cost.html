What a professional mosquito treatment includes

A standard barrier spray treatment applies residual insecticide (typically bifenthrin or permethrin) to all vegetation where mosquitoes rest during the day — shrubs, tall grass, tree leaves, and the shaded understory of landscaping. Treatment takes 20–45 minutes and reduces mosquito activity for 3–4 weeks. Some companies also treat standing water sources with larvicides.

How to reduce mosquitoes without spraying

  • Eliminate standing water — the biggest lever. Mosquitoes breed in as little as a teaspoon of water: empty saucers under plants, birdbaths (change weekly), clogged gutters, tires, and low spots in the lawn
  • Mosquito dunks — BTI (biological) larvicide, safe for ponds, birdbaths, and rain barrels; kills larvae before they hatch
  • Yard fans — mosquitoes are weak fliers; a large fan on a deck or patio significantly reduces landing rates

Is a seasonal mosquito program worth it?

Do the math: a 6-treatment seasonal program at $500 for 5 months of significantly reduced mosquito activity equals roughly $100/month. Compare that to the cost of not using your backyard — cancelled outdoor gatherings, reduced evenings outside, tick and disease risk. In states with long mosquito seasons (Texas, Florida, Georgia, Carolinas), most homeowners find the program a strong value. In cooler northern climates, a 3-4 treatment abbreviated program at $300–$400 may be more appropriate.

Frequently asked questions

How much does mosquito yard treatment cost?

A single professional mosquito barrier treatment costs $150–$400. Seasonal programs covering 6–8 bi-weekly treatments (May–October) run $400–$700. Monthly service during peak season costs $40–$100/visit.

How long does mosquito treatment last?

A single barrier spray treatment reduces mosquito activity for approximately 3–4 weeks, depending on rainfall and weather. Heavy rain can reduce effectiveness to 2 weeks. Bi-weekly service maintains consistent control throughout the season.

What is the most effective mosquito control for yards?

A combination approach works best: professional barrier spray + source elimination (removing standing water) + larvicide treatment of any standing water that can't be eliminated. Barrier spray alone without addressing breeding sites has reduced effectiveness in properties with significant standing water.

Does mosquito spray harm bees or pets?

Bifenthrin and pyrethrin-based mosquito treatments are toxic to bees while wet. Reputable companies spray at dusk when bees are less active and avoid blooming plants. After the spray dries (typically 30–60 minutes), exposure risk to pets and children is minimal at applied concentrations.
