How Often Should You Get Pest Control?

The right service frequency depends on where you live, your home's history, and which pests you're dealing with. Here's the honest breakdown.

Updated April 2026  ·  PestControlQuotesNow.com

The short answerQuarterly service (4 visits/year) is the right frequency for most homeowners in pest-active regions. Monthly service is recommended for severe infestations or high-risk properties. Annual service may be sufficient for low-pest regions or recently treated homes in dry climates. One-time visits work for isolated, non-recurring problems.

Why quarterly service is the industry standard

Most pest control plans are built around quarterly visits because pest pressures follow seasonal patterns: spring brings ants and termite swarms; summer brings mosquitoes, wasps, and outdoor pests moving inside; fall brings rodents seeking warmth as temperatures drop; winter brings overwintering pests in wall voids. A quarterly schedule creates a fresh barrier treatment for each seasonal wave.

Signs you need more frequent service

  • You're seeing pests between scheduled visits with increasing frequency
  • You have an active, unsolved infestation that regular service hasn't resolved
  • You live in a dense multi-unit building where re-infestation from neighbors is ongoing
  • You have a restaurant, food business, or other commercial operation with regulatory requirements

Maximizing the value of quarterly service

The callback guarantee that comes with most quarterly plans is the most underused feature. If you see any pest activity between scheduled visits, call your company — the callback visit is typically free under the plan. Don't wait for your scheduled visit. Early intervention between visits prevents minor activity from becoming a full infestation.

Frequently asked questions

How often should you get pest control done?

Quarterly service (4 visits/year) is the standard recommendation for most US homeowners. Monthly service is appropriate for active infestations. Annual service may be sufficient in low-pest, arid climates. The right frequency depends on your climate, home type, and past pest history.

Is monthly pest control necessary?

Monthly pest control is necessary for active infestations that require multiple treatments to break breeding cycles (especially German cockroaches and bed bugs), high-density multi-unit buildings with persistent re-infestation, and commercial food-service establishments with regulatory requirements. For most residential properties, quarterly is sufficient.

Does pest control work if you only do it once a year?

Annual service provides some protection but is often insufficient for maintaining effective perimeter barriers throughout the year, especially in warm climates. Residual insecticide treatments typically break down within 60–90 days. Annual service is more appropriate as a supplemental treatment after a pest-free period, not as ongoing prevention.

How long after pest control treatment should you wait before going inside?

For liquid barrier treatments: 30–60 minutes to dry, then it's safe to re-enter. For indoor treatments: follow the technician's specific guidance — typically 2–4 hours for most treatments. For fumigation: the home must air out for 24–72 hours after tent removal. Always ask your technician for re-entry guidance specific to the products used.
