How to Get Rid of Cockroaches: What Actually Works

Cockroaches are among the most resilient pests on earth. Most OTC products fail. Here's the protocol that actually eliminates infestations.

Updated April 2026  ·  PestControlQuotesNow.com

The #1 ruleRoach sprays kill on contact but don't reach the 95%+ of the infestation hiding in cracks, wall voids, and appliances. Professional-grade gel bait + insect growth regulators (IGRs) is the most effective treatment for German cockroaches (the most common species). The key: no spray near bait, multiple small bait placements, and treatment of harboring sites.

The gel bait protocol that actually works

For German cockroaches (the most common indoor species), the proven treatment protocol is:

  1. Apply gel bait in small pea-sized amounts in cracks, hinges, corners under appliances, and inside cabinets — not in open areas. Products: Advion Cockroach Gel (indoxacarb) or Vendetta Plus (abamectin).
  2. Apply IGR (insect growth regulator) such as Gentrol (hydroprene) — this prevents immature roaches from reproducing, breaking the breeding cycle. Apply to wall voids and harboring areas.
  3. Do NOT spray near bait — insecticide sprays contaminate bait and cause roaches to avoid it. This is the #1 DIY mistake.
  4. Remove food sources — store all food in sealed containers, fix any plumbing leaks, clean under appliances.
  5. Allow 2–4 weeks for full effect. Bait is slow-acting by design — it needs to be carried back and shared before killing.

When DIY fails: signs you need a professional

  • You see roaches during daytime — this indicates extreme overcrowding; the infestation has outgrown hiding spots
  • Multiple rooms or building-wide activity — often indicates a structural or shared-wall problem
  • You've tried gel bait for 4+ weeks with no reduction — incorrect placement or the wrong species protocol
  • Apartment/multi-unit building — roaches can re-infest from adjacent units faster than DIY treatment can address

Frequently asked questions

Why do I keep getting cockroaches after treatment?

Re-infestation usually comes from one of three sources: eggs hatching (roach eggs are resistant to many insecticides — IGR treatment is essential to break the cycle), re-entry from outside (American cockroaches), or shared-wall re-infestation from adjacent units in apartments. A callback inspection should identify which is occurring.

Do cockroaches come back after extermination?

Not if the treatment was thorough and root causes are addressed. German cockroach infestations can recur from unhatched eggs (requires IGR treatment) or from external introduction. American cockroach issues recur if entry points aren't sealed. Professional treatment should include a warranty or callback provision.

What kills cockroaches instantly?

Contact insecticides (sprays, boric acid powder) kill individual roaches on contact but don't reach the colony hiding in cracks and voids. For immediate knockdown: insecticide spray. For permanent elimination: gel bait + IGR protocol over 2–4 weeks.

Are cockroaches dangerous?

Yes. Cockroaches spread pathogens by contaminating food preparation surfaces and utensils. Cockroach allergens are a significant trigger for asthma, especially in children. The WHO classifies cockroaches as public health pests because of their role in spreading bacteria including Salmonella, E. coli, and Staphylococcus.
