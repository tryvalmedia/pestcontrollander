What's included in a pest control visit

A standard professional visit typically includes: exterior perimeter inspection and treatment, interior crack-and-crevice treatment, garage and entry point treatment, and a technician report on activity observed. Initial visits are more thorough and take longer — 45–90 minutes. Recurring visits are often 20–40 minutes focused on treatment and monitoring.

One-time vs. recurring: which is better value?

A homeowner in a pest-active region who deals with ants in spring, wasps in summer, and mice in fall would pay $200–$350 each time as one-off visits — totaling $600–$1,050 annually. An annual service plan covering all three costs $300–$600, often with free callback visits if pests return. For most homeowners who deal with multiple pest types per year, recurring plans win on value.

Watch for deceptive "initial offer" pricingSome companies advertise low initial visit prices ($29–$49) to get you signed up for a long-term contract. Read the fine print — annual contracts with penalty clauses can lock you in at prices higher than stated. Always ask for the full annual contract cost before signing.

What drives pest control prices up

  • Home size: Most companies charge more above 2,000–2,500 sq ft — typically $25–$50 per additional 1,000 sq ft
  • Severity: Active infestations require more product, more time, and often follow-up visits
  • Access difficulty: Crawl spaces, attics, and multi-story structures increase labor
  • Pest type: Termites and bed bugs require specialized equipment and protocols
  • Geography: NYC, LA, Boston, and coastal markets average 25–40% above national pricing

Frequently asked questions

How much does pest control cost per month?

Monthly pest control service costs $40–$70 per visit after an initial treatment of $150–$300. Annual plans with quarterly visits are $300–$900/year — roughly $25–$75/month — and usually offer better value than monthly service for most households.

Is it worth paying for pest control?

For most homeowners, yes. A quarterly service plan at $400–$600/year prevents the cost of dealing with active infestations (which can run $300–$2,000+ for serious pests like termites or bed bugs). The prevention value typically exceeds the annual plan cost for homes in pest-active regions.

How much is a one-time pest control visit?

One-time pest control visits cost $100–$300 for common pests (ants, roaches, spiders) and $150–$600 for rodents. Specialty pests like bed bugs and termites are priced separately and can run $500–$3,000+ depending on the extent of infestation and treatment method.

How often should you get pest control?

Most pest control companies recommend quarterly service (4 visits/year) for standard prevention. Monthly service is recommended for persistent infestations or high-risk properties. One-time visits are appropriate for isolated, single-pest issues with no history of recurrence.
