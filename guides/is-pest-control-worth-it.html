Quarterly service or just call when there's a problem? Here's the actual cost-benefit analysis — including when DIY is legitimately fine.

When pest control is clearly worth it

  • You're in a high-pest climate — Gulf Coast, Southeast, or anywhere with year-round warmth and humidity
  • You've had termites previously — ongoing monitoring warranty is essentially structural insurance
  • You live in a multi-unit building — you can do everything right and still get roaches or bed bugs from neighboring units
  • You have small children or pets — professional-grade products with proper application are safer than improper DIY chemical use
  • You have a crawl space or basement — these are harborage zones for rodents, termites, and moisture pests
  • You've dealt with the same pest problem 2+ consecutive years — recurring problems have a structural or environmental cause that DIY can't address

When DIY pest control is genuinely enough

  • You're in a low-pest region (arid climates, newer construction, above ground floor in a building)
  • You deal with 1–2 predictable, minor seasonal pest incidents per year
  • The pests are ants or spiders with a single, identifiable entry point you can seal and treat
  • You're willing to do a thorough job — finding and sealing entry points, using correct products correctly, monitoring regularly

The case for quarterly plans specifically

The standard professional service structure — initial treatment + quarterly visits with unlimited callback visits — is designed to catch problems early. A qualified technician will spot signs of termite activity, rodent entry points, or early cockroach establishment before they become expensive problems. This early detection value is hard to replicate with DIY because most homeowners don't know what to look for.

Frequently asked questions

Is quarterly pest control worth it?

For most homeowners in pest-active regions, quarterly service at $400–$700/year is worth it because it prevents the far higher cost of active infestations (termites, bed bugs, rodents) and typically includes unlimited callback visits between treatments. The value case is strongest in warm, humid climates and homes with crawl spaces or basements.

Can I do my own pest control?

Yes, for minor and surface-level pest problems. Store-bought gel bait works reasonably well for ants. Snap traps handle light rodent activity. OTC products have limitations though: they don't have professional-grade actives for resistant populations, don't address root causes like entry points and harborage sites, and don't include the monitoring that catches problems early.

Is professional pest control safer than DIY?

Professional technicians apply EPA-registered products at correct concentrations and know how to apply them safely around children, pets, and food preparation areas. Improper DIY application of concentrated insecticides or rodenticides can be more dangerous than professional treatment. Most professional products used today are low-toxicity to mammals at applied concentrations.

What pests does a standard pest control plan cover?

Standard quarterly plans typically cover: ants, cockroaches, spiders, earwigs, silverfish, centipedes, and exterior perimeter treatment. Most plans add rodent monitoring as an add-on. Termites, bed bugs, mosquitoes, and wildlife are usually priced separately due to specialized treatment requirements.
